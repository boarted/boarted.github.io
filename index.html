Boarting

a textboard is the precursor to what we now know as the 'imageboard', a kind of anonymous bulletin board based around the posting of images. with a textboard, there are no images, just text. as such, given the anonymity, group identity is contained entirely in the language of the posts, the particular syntagmatic choices / general 'voice' one puts on to signal to other people that - YES. I am anonymous too. anagram of textboard is boarted. for this project, i wanted to boart the textboard experience.

aphasia is an impairment in speech. i occasionally suffer from it. unsurprisingly, just as a broken machine shows you how to fix it, aphasia has long been an object of study for linguists. roman jakobson placed aphasics in two categories in order to make larger observations about how we put words together to make language. there were those who lacked paradigmatic faculties, and those who lacked syntagmatic ones. these were the similarity disorder and the contiguity disorder. it's quite simple to imagine someone without paradigmatic faculties (the similarity disorder). this is marked by inappropriate lexicon. the Jabberwocky comes to mind. just substitute words. uhe contiguity disorder is a deficiency of syntax. words are appropriate, they come out, but in the wrong order.

by boarting, i wanted to make the textboard voice strange. i wanted to twist it in a way that let me say 'yes, this is language. this is textboard language.' this would be simple to simulated by replacing words and jumbling their order. but i wanted more. real aphasia is not simply divided into two disordersm, it combines the two. i wanted to do it through the interface of the imageboard itself, to make the computer croak back to me the language id known. so i trained a neural network on several months worth of posts scraped manually from a textboard for a few days. this was a specially written network, it was 'forgetful', which is to say, it suffered from a disorder that caused its normally otherworldly ability to make language out of nothing, rather aphasic. that's /boart/!
